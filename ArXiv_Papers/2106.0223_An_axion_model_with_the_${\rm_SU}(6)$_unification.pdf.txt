An axion model with the SU(6) unification
Ning Chen,1,  Yutong Liu,1,  and Zhaolong Teng1,  1School of Physics, Nankai University, Tianjin, 300071, China
We propose an axion model with the SU(6) unification symmetry. Such a unification model can lead to an automatic global U(1)PQ symmetry from two copies of anti-fundamental fermions. The SU(6) is first broken to the extended symmetry of SU(3)c  SU(3)L  U(1)N , and the Higgs fields for the sequential symmetry breaking contain the physical axion fields. The low-energy theory at the electroweak scale is described by the type-II two-Higgs-doublet model. The physical axion satisfies the Peccei-Quinn quality condition without much fine-tuning of the dimension-6 operator, with the mass range of  O(10-3) - O(0.1) eV. The axion-photon coupling of ga is identical to the type-I DFSZ scenario, and can be probed in the upcoming axion search experiments of LAMPOST and IAXO in the high mass region.

The experimental conflict with the O(1) expectation of the  parameter, is known as the strong CP problem. The most promising solution is believed to be Peccei-Quinn (PQ) mechanism [2­4], where a dynamical axion field of a transforming under the global U(1)PQ symmetry is introduced to shift the  parameter.
Two benchmark scenarios of the invisible axion models and their varieties are known as the Kim-ShifmanVainshtein-Zakharov (KSVZ) model [5, 6] and the DineFischler-Srednicki-Zhitnitsky (DFSZ) model [7, 8]. The grand unification theories (GUT) were considered as framework for the axion models based on the SU(5) [9] and SO(10) [10] unifications. Later, other varieties based on the SU(5) [11­20], SO(10) [18, 21­26], and E6 [27] were also studied. In most of the previous SU(5) and SO(10) GUT model buildings, the global U(1)PQ symmetry commutes with the GUT group. This leads to the PQ quality problem [11, 28­32], where the global U(1)PQ symmetry should be a good symmetry at sufficiently high scale. It is therefore desirable to have the PQ symmetry arise automatically, and this was discussed in the context of various GUTs [11, 26], as well as models with extended gauge symmetries [33­46].
In this Letter, we propose an SU(6) GUT with a highquality U(1)PQ. This was first noted by Dimopoulos, Raby and Susskind in the study of the confining theories [47] that an anomaly-free SU(N + 4) gauge theory with N chiral fermions in the anti-fundamental representation and one chiral fermion in the rank-2 antisymmetric representation enjoys a global symmetry of

Gglobal = U(N)F = SU(N)F  U(1)F ,

(2)

when N  2. Our key assumption is to take the SU(6) as the gauge group of the GUT and to identify U(1)F as the U(1)PQ in Eq. (2). Historically, the SU(6) GUT was studied in various contexts [48­62], and found to alleviate the intrinsic doublet-triplet splitting problem [63­67] and the proton lifetime constraint [49, 54, 60] in the GeorgiGlashow SU(5) GUT [68].
Our main observation is that the axion decay constant can be in the range of fa  O(108) - O(1010) GeV without much fine-tuning of the leading PQ-breaking operator with d = 6, by gauging the SU(2)F symmetry and imposing a discrete Z4 symmetry. Thus, the axion arising from the minimal SU(6) model is of high quality. Several other features of the minimal SU(6) model include: (i) predictions of the KSVZ vector-like D-quarks to form D-hadrons through renormalizable operators, with lifetime of D  10-36 sec to avoid the cosmological constraints, (ii) the extended SU(3)c  SU(3)L  U(1)N symmetry at v331  O(109) - O(1011) GeV, which is known as the 331 model [62, 69­74], (iii) prediction of the typeII two-Higgs-doublet model (2HDM) at the electroweak (EW) scale, (iv) additional heavy neutrinos with Majorana mass terms for the type-I seesaw mechanism.

Conclusions.­ We have put forth an SU(6) unification model with a high-quality axion for the strong CP problem of the QCD. The key observation is to identify the Abelian component of the global symmetry in Eq. (2) as the U(1)PQ, and the SU(6) GUT is the minimal realization. Indeed, a high-quality axion is found as the linear combination from the phase fields of 6IHI and 21H. The conventional constraints to the KSVZ models, such as the electric charges, the masses, and the related hadron lifetime become well predicted with the PQ quality constraints. Our minimal SU(6) model further reduces the preferred axion decay constant in the window of 108 GeV fa 1010 GeV, with moderate fine-tuning of the dimension-6 PQ-breaking operator. Such a prediction turns out to be predictable in the upcoming axion search experiments of LAMPOST and IAXO. The theoretical motivation and phenomenological implications of this model drive future efforts in the class of GUT models beyond the SU(5) and SO(10).
